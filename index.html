✨ About Holi ✨

Holi is one of the most celebrated festivals in India, known as the "Festival of Colors" or "Festival of Spring."


It marks the arrival of spring, the end of winter, and celebrates the victory of good over evil. People celebrate by throwing colored powder (gulal) and water at each other, singing, dancing, and enjoying festive foods.


The festival is also a time to forgive, forget grievances, and repair broken relationships.

🎉 Holi Fun Facts 🎉

🔥
Holika Dahan
The night before Holi, bonfires are lit to symbolize the burning of evil spirits and the triumph of good over evil.
🎨
Natural Colors
Traditionally, Holi colors were made from natural sources like turmeric, flowers, and herbs.
🍬
Festive Foods
Special treats like gujiya, mathri, and thandai are prepared and shared during Holi celebrations.
🌍
Global Celebration
Holi is celebrated not just in India, but around the world by Indian communities and color festival enthusiasts!
